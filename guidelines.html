Submission Guidelines

Standards and expectations for Pillar & Pew contributors

Overview

Pillar & Pew publishes bold, principled writing across philosophy, Catholic thought, firearms, and culture. We value clarity, intellectual rigor, and writers who aren't afraid to take a stand. If you can think deeply and write clearly, we want to hear from you.

We welcome both established writers and talented newcomers. What matters most is the quality of your thinking and your ability to communicate it effectively.

What We Publish

Philosophy & Classical Thought

Deep dives into philosophical questions, analysis of classical thinkers (Aquinas, Aristotle, Augustine, etc.), applications of natural law theory, metaphysics, epistemology, and ethics. We favor rigorous thinking presented accessibly.

Catholic & Orthodox Thought

Apologetics, theological reflection, critiques of modern heresies, defense of traditional Christian teaching, practical spirituality, and examinations of Church history. We value orthodoxy without being dry or academic.

Firearms, Gear & Shooting Sports

Practical reviews, training advice, gear comparisons, range reports, concealed carry considerations, self-defense philosophy, and hot takes on firearms culture. Technical expertise welcome, but write for engaged enthusiasts, not just operators.

Cultural Commentary

Sharp analysis of current events, media critique, examinations of cultural trends, and principled takes on politics and society. We value original insight over rehashing mainstream talking points.

Writing Standards

Required Elements

  • Clear thesis: Know what you're arguing and state it plainly
  • Logical structure: Arguments should build systematically
  • Evidence & citations: Back up claims with sources when needed
  • Engaging prose: Write clearly, but don't be boring
  • Original thinking: Add something new to the conversation

Tone & Style

We appreciate boldness, but avoid needless combativeness. Make your case forcefully, but charitably engage opposing views. Think "intellectual confidence" rather than "internet anger."

Write for intelligent readers who value substance. Avoid academic jargon when plain English works better, but don't dumb things down. Our audience can handle complexity.

Technical Requirements

Article Length

  • • Standard articles: 1,200-2,500 words
  • • Deep dives: 2,500-5,000 words
  • • Quick takes: 600-1,200 words

Format

  • • Microsoft Word (.docx) or Google Docs
  • • 12pt font, standard margins
  • • Include word count in header
  • • Properly formatted citations

Images & Media

  • • Suggest header image (optional)
  • • Include image credits/sources
  • • High-resolution preferred (1200px+ wide)
  • • We may source our own images

Author Bio

  • • 2-3 sentence bio
  • • Social media handles (optional)
  • • Website or portfolio link (optional)
  • • Photo or headshot (optional)

What We Don't Publish

  • Poorly researched or factually incorrect content
  • Plagiarism or insufficiently cited work
  • Content that contradicts core Catholic/Orthodox teaching (for theology pieces)
  • Unsubstantiated conspiracy theories
  • Gratuitous vulgarity or ad hominem attacks
  • Promotional content or thinly veiled advertising
  • Previously published work (unless you own all rights)

Submission Process

1

Prepare Your Article

Review these guidelines thoroughly and ensure your piece meets our standards.

2

Email Your Submission

Send your article to hello@pillarandpew.com with:

  • • Subject line: "Article Submission: [Your Title]"
  • • Attached Word doc or link to Google Doc
  • • Brief cover email introducing yourself
  • • Author bio and contact information
3

Editorial Review

We'll review your submission within 2-3 weeks. Due to volume, we can only respond to pieces we're interested in publishing. If accepted, we may suggest edits.

4

Publication

Once finalized, your article will be published on our site. We'll notify you and share the link. You're free to promote it on your own channels.

Rights & Compensation

Currently, Pillar & Pew is a non-paying publication. We're building our audience and establishing our platform. Contributors retain full rights to their work and may republish elsewhere after publication on our site.

We grant you a non-exclusive license to publish your article. You keep all rights and can use your piece however you wish (in a book, on your blog, etc.). We simply ask that you credit Pillar & Pew as the original publisher.

As we grow, we plan to implement compensation for contributors. Early contributors will be prioritized for paid opportunities.

Questions?

If anything is unclear or you have specific questions about whether your piece is a good fit, don't hesitate to reach out.
